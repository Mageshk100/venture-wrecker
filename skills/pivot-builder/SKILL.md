# Pivot Builder

The roast is done. Now comes the valuable part: finding the real business hiding inside the broken idea. Every bad pitch has a better one inside it. Your job is to find it.

## How to Execute This Skill

1. **Extract the core insight** — Strip away the bad execution and find what the founder actually understood:
   - What real pain were they sensing?
   - What market signal did they pick up on?
   - What unique perspective do they have that most people miss?

2. **Identify the pivot type** — Which kind of pivot applies here?
   - **Zoom in** — Too broad. Focus on one specific customer or use case
   - **Zoom out** — Too narrow. The real product is the platform, not the feature
   - **Customer pivot** — Right solution, wrong buyer. Who actually needs this?
   - **Channel pivot** — Right product, wrong distribution. How should it actually reach people?
   - **Business model pivot** — Right market, wrong monetization. How does money actually flow?
   - **Technology pivot** — The tech is right but solving the wrong problem with it
   - **Timing pivot** — Great idea, wrong moment. What needs to be true first?

3. **Build the stronger version** — Construct the pivot concretely:
   - New one-line description
   - New target customer (specific, named if possible)
   - New go-to-market approach
   - Why this version is fundable when the original wasn't

4. **Define the first 90 days** — What does the founder do Monday morning?
   - 3 specific, actionable steps to validate the pivot
   - What does success look like at 30/60/90 days?

## Output Format

**The Hidden Insight:**
[What the founder actually got right — the seed of the real business]

**The Pivot:**
[Type of pivot + new one-line description of the stronger version]

**Why This Version Works:**
[3 specific reasons the pivot is stronger than the original]

**New Target Customer:**
[Named, specific, reachable — not "enterprises" or "millennials"]

**First 90 Days:**
[3 concrete actions. What to build, who to talk to, what to measure]
