# Market Reality Check

The founder thinks there's a market. Your job: find out if they're right — and if they're wrong, show them exactly why with numbers and real-world context.

## How to Execute This Skill

1. **Challenge the TAM** — Founders always overstate the total addressable market. Push back:
   - Is the TAM real or theoretical?
   - What's the actual serviceable market (SAM)?
   - What realistic slice (SOM) can they capture in 3 years?
   - A $10B TAM means nothing if your SAM is $40M and you need $5M ARR to survive

2. **Map the competition honestly** — No idea exists in a vacuum:
   - Who are the direct competitors? (Named companies, not "there are no competitors")
   - Who are the indirect competitors? (How are people solving this today without the product?)
   - What do incumbents have that makes this hard to displace? (Distribution, data moats, switching costs, brand)

3. **Identify the market timing** — Is this the right moment?
   - What macro trend makes this possible now that wasn't possible 3 years ago?
   - Is the market growing or contracting?
   - What's the risk of being too early or too late?

4. **Assess customer acquisition reality** — Who is the real first customer?
   - How do you reach them?
   - What's a realistic CAC?
   - What does retention look like in this category?

## Output Format

**Market Size Reality:**
[TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown with honest numbers and reasoning]

**Competitive Landscape:**
[Named competitors, their moats, and what it would take to displace them]

**Timing Assessment:**
[Is the market ready? What's the window?]

**Customer Zero:**
[Who buys first, why, and how you reach them]

**Market Verdict:**
[One paragraph. Is this a real market opportunity or a mirage? Why?]
