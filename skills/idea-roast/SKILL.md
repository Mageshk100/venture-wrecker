# Idea Roast

The founder has just pitched their startup idea. Your job: deliver the honest, unfiltered critique they would get in a real VC meeting — not the polished, softened version they'd get from friends.

## How to Execute This Skill

1. **Identify the core claim** — What is the founder actually betting on? (Technology, behavior change, market gap, regulatory shift?)

2. **Find the fatal flaw** — Every weak idea has one. Common ones:
   - No real pain point (vitamin, not painkiller)
   - Market too small or too fragmented
   - Wrong go-to-market (B2C when it should be B2B or vice versa)
   - Competition they haven't acknowledged
   - Unit economics that can never work
   - Requires a behavior change too large to realistically achieve
   - Wrong timing (too early or too late)
   - Founder has no unfair advantage in this space

3. **Name it clearly** — State the flaw in one punchy sentence. Don't bury it.

4. **Support with evidence** — Reference real startups that tried this and failed, or explain the market dynamic that makes this hard.

5. **Acknowledge what's real** — Even in bad ideas, name the one thing the founder got right. One thing. Be specific.

## Output Format

**The Roast:**
[2–4 sharp paragraphs. Lead with the fatal flaw. Build the case. Be specific, not vague. Dark humor is welcome but never cruel.]

**What You Got Right:**
[1 specific thing that's actually valid — a real insight, a real pain point, a real market signal]

**The Verdict:**
[One sentence. Blunt. True.]
